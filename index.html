Projects

CECL Credit Risk Modeling

Objective: Build a CECL-compliant framework to estimate lifetime expected credit losses for financial institutions.

Approach: Used logistic regression to estimate probability of default and ARIMAX for time-series forecasting under baseline and stress scenarios. Selected these models for interpretability and regulatory transparency, even though more complex ML models could slightly improve accuracy.

Outcome: Produced explainable loss forecasts aligned with CECL standards. Enabled scenario testing using GDP, unemployment, and delinquency rates to support stress testing requirements.

Fair Lending Analysis – GBM & EBM

Objective: Detect and remediate demographic bias in mortgage approvals to meet fair lending regulations (ECOA, FHA).

Approach: Compared GBM, XGBoost, and Explainable Boosting Machines (EBM). Chose EBM for interpretability and fairness testing using SHAP values and Adverse Impact Ratio (AIR). Tradeoff: slightly lower AUC than GBM, but better compliance readiness.

Outcome: Achieved balanced performance (Test AUC = 0.83) while reducing bias against female and Black applicants. Demonstrated how explainable models can align AI with responsible lending practices.

Iowa Liquor Sales Market Entry

Objective: Guide market entry strategy for a retail client by analyzing statewide liquor sales data.

Approach: Deployed PostgreSQL on AWS RDS to process 20M+ sales records. Modeled store performance and demographics, then connected results to Power BI dashboards for interactive insights. Tradeoff: focused on scalability and transparency instead of highly complex ML models.

Outcome: Identified profitable counties and store locations, providing data-driven evidence to support the client’s Class E liquor license application in Iowa.

Capital Bikeshare Demand Forecasting

Objective: Optimize fleet allocation by forecasting daily bike pickups and drop-offs across stations.

Approach: Tested regression models under a cost-based decision framework. Chose LASSO for feature selection and multicollinearity control, while comparing with Ridge regression for variance handling. Assumption: weather and trip history were the strongest predictors of demand.

Outcome: LASSO achieved RMSE = 4.40 and R² = 0.85, enabling accurate and explainable forecasts that could reduce rebalancing inefficiencies across the system.
